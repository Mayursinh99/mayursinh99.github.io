Phishing Awareness & Workshop

Phishing is a type of online scam where attackers send fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources in order to trick people into giving away sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, and other personal details. It is important to be aware of phishing attacks and take steps to protect yourself.

Phishing is a type of online scam where attackers send fake emails or messages that appear to be from legitimate sources in order to trick people into giving away sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, and other personal details.
There are several steps you can take to protect yourself from phishing attacks:
  • Be cautious when receiving emails or messages from unfamiliar sources, or those that contain urgent or threatening language.
  • Look for signs that the email or message may be fake, such as typos, poor grammar, or mismatched logos or branding.
  • Be careful when clicking on links in emails or messages, even if they appear to be from a legitimate source. It is best to navigate directly to a website by typing the URL into your browser rather than clicking on a link.
  • Keep your software and security tools up to date, as these can help protect you from phishing attacks and other online threats.
  • Be cautious when entering personal or financial information online, and make sure the website is secure (look for the "https" in the URL and a padlock icon).
  • Consider using two-factor authentication for added security. This requires you to enter a code sent to your phone or email in addition to your password
